My Vision

I want to play a pivotal role in bringing back manufacturing into Canada. This is possible through automation by integrating technology, such as artificial intelligence, robotics, and mechanics.

COVID taught us a harsh lesson that the Canadian economy is too dependent on imports, such as from China. This is partially because of China's large population giving them a competitive cost advantage in manufacturing. The same cannot be said for Canada, as we have a small population base, and if this country needs manufacturing, it should be done through the use of automation of processes and machinery. Time and time again, innovations have caused disruptions, and with the most recent surge of integrated artificial intelligence systems, there is a vast scope of attaining competitive advantage in cost. Therefore, the importance of mechatronics and robotics in Canada will have an even brighter future than today.

In pursuit of this dream, I began the journey in 2020, where I learned programming and built upon my engineering skills by joining my school's robotics team. Moreover, I founded a STEM club to introduce junior students to the world of mechatronics. Currently, I am looking to join a world-renowned University that will help me achieve my dream.
