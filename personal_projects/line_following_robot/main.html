Arduino Line Following Robot

As founder of the Arduino club, I decided that it would be a great idea if we ended the first academic year with a simple project that bring all acquired concepts together. Every week, we learnt something about a new Arduino component and by the end of the year, everybody had a solid understanding of how IR sensors, motor drivers and DC motors worked.

The most difficult part of the project was preparing a reasonable design. Due to conflict of interests and very less time, we decided to use a wooden block as the base of the robot. Furthermore, 2 IR sensors were to be mounted on the right and left front sides respectively. The front of the robot was supported by a toothbrush while the back was driven by 2 DC motors, all being controlled from one L298N motor driver. The Arduino board itself was powered through a power bank.



I wanted my club members to show me what they have learned throughout the semester, so I helped them as little as I can. When they were desperate, I aided them to get out of the problem. The biggest problem we faced was when our IR sensors were not reading values consistently. However, this problem was fixed within a few minutes after we got a teacher to screw the IR sensors properly.

Most of the construction was completed by my good friends, namely Shreekavin Umasankar, Muhammad Saajid, Muhammad Nomaan and Supreet Pamma. The programming was controlled by Sabarisan Sabaratnam and Bilal Yasini. There were other members in the club as well, but they were not main contributors to the final project.

Robot successfully following the track. Additional media can be found at @st_josephss_arduino on Instagram.

The project was completed on the last meeting of the year. The amount of relief felt after encountering numerous obstacles was huge and it was a moment for the dedicated club members to congratualte themselves for completing one of the many big projects that they will pursue in their lives.

The source code for this project can be found at my GitHub.
