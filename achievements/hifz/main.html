Complete Memorization of the Holy Qur'an - Word by Word

This tedious, tiresome and lengthy journey began in July 2017 on a random Tuesday night when I was feeling spiritually uplifted. I felt the need to start memorizing portions of the Qur'an out of nowhere. At first, I was easily accomplishing the self-assigned task to the extent where I asked my parents to enroll me in a Madrasah (an Islamic school) for 2 months. In this time, I performed quite well and it increased my hopes of completing my memorization journey within the next 2-3 years.

The Qur'an is split into 30 parts (roughly 604 pages). I had memorized 9 of these in my first year and when my second year was about to begin, my family decided to move to Canada from Saudi Arabia. Finding a Madrasah in Canada was difficult, but after 3 long months, my parents enrolled me in Masjid Al Farooq. It was in this Madrasah, where I revised my final verses before the completion of this jounrey. On April 17, 2020, I was granted the title of 'Hafiz', which is an Arabic term for 'Protector'.

It was not an easy accomplishment by any stretch of the imagination. The road was full of ups and downs, and my parents were present for me when I felt defeated. This act was done for the sake of God Almighty, and my parents motivated me throughout the journey for which I am ever grateful.
